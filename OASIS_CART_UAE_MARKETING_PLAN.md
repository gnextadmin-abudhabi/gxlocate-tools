# Oasis Cart UAE Growth & Marketing Plan

**Prepared:** 18 July 2026  
**Recommended commitment:** 3 months  
**Market:** United Arab Emirates  
**Currency:** AED; fees below exclude 5% VAT

## 1. Executive recommendation

Oasis Cart is a new iOS shopping marketplace covering fashion, electronics, home, beauty, and daily essentials. Its strongest near-term opportunity is not to advertise every category equally. The first 90 days should identify 2–3 “hero” categories and 10–20 hero products with the best combination of demand, margin, stock depth, delivery reliability, and promotional appeal.

The lowest-risk entry option is **Budgetical: AED 6,500 per month**—AED 3,000 agency retainer plus AED 3,500 media spend. The **Medium option at AED 12,000 per month** is the recommended growth plan once tracking, fulfilment, product feed quality, and initial purchase economics are validated. A flexible Custom option is also available for budgets or requirements outside the three fixed packages.

Success will be measured by **first purchases, cost per first purchase, revenue, repeat purchase rate, and contribution margin**, not app installs alone. The first quarter is a controlled market-validation phase; scaling happens only when product economics and tracking support it.

## 2. Current-state findings

The App Store listing presents Oasis Cart as a free shopping companion with:

- Fashion, electronics, home, beauty, and other categories
- Secure checkout and Apple Pay
- Deals and discounts
- Wishlist, search and filters
- Order tracking and personalized recommendations

The app is English-only in the listing, has no visible ratings overview, and currently shows only an iOS destination. These create four immediate marketing priorities:

1. Build trust through product demonstrations, delivery proof, customer service visibility, clear returns information, and authentic reviews.
2. Improve App Store conversion with stronger screenshots, category-specific benefits, bilingual assets where supported, and regular review generation.
3. Install app analytics before scaling so paid media can optimize toward purchase value rather than low-quality installs.
4. Establish a focused reason to choose Oasis Cart over large UAE marketplaces: exclusive products, local relevance, faster delivery, price advantage, bundles, or superior service.

## 3. Objectives for the first 90 days

### Month 1 — Foundation and controlled launch

- Complete analytics, attribution, catalogue/feed, and channel setup
- Select hero categories/products using margin, stock, and fulfilment data
- Create the first bilingual content and advertising asset bank
- Launch prospecting and retargeting tests
- Establish baseline CPI, registration rate, first-purchase CPA, AOV, and ROAS

### Month 2 — Product/creative validation

- Shift budget toward winning product, audience, and creative combinations
- Test first-order incentives, bundles, free-delivery thresholds, and urgency
- Build purchaser lookalikes when volume permits
- Add lifecycle messages for browse, wishlist, cart, purchase, and replenishment

### Month 3 — Scale and retention

- Increase spend only on campaigns meeting contribution-margin targets
- Expand winning categories and introduce new products in controlled waves
- Launch repeat-purchase, cross-sell, referral, and review campaigns
- Present a quarter-two growth plan based on real cohort economics

## 4. Priority UAE audiences

Age targeting starts at **18+** and should follow actual purchaser data rather than assumptions after the first month.

| Audience | Need/intent | Initial message | Best channels |
|---|---|---|---|
| UAE deal seekers, 18–44 | Value, offers, convenient discovery | Exclusive app deals, bundles, limited drops | Meta, TikTok, Google App |
| Young professionals | Fast mobile shopping and delivery | Everything you need in a few taps | Instagram, YouTube, Google |
| Families and household buyers | Home, essentials, repeat purchases | Practical value, reliable delivery, easy tracking | Facebook, Instagram, Google |
| Fashion and beauty shoppers | Discovery, trends, social proof | New arrivals, creator demos, before/after or styling | Instagram, TikTok |
| Electronics shoppers | Specifications, trust, price comparison | Clear benefits, warranty/returns, competitive offer | Google, YouTube, Meta retargeting |
| Existing app users | Wishlist, cart, replenishment | Back in stock, price drop, cart reminder, reorder | Meta retargeting, push, email/SMS |

**Geography:** Start UAE-wide only if delivery speed, fees, COD/payment methods, and stock are consistent. Otherwise launch by proven service zones—typically Dubai, Abu Dhabi, and Sharjah first—then expand. Reporting must be split by emirate, language, new/returning buyer, category, and iOS version/device where useful.

**Language:** English plus natural UAE-relevant Arabic. Separate creative variants are preferable to crowded bilingual artwork. Add Hindi/Urdu only after customer and sales data supports it.

## 5. Channel strategy

### Organic social media

Primary channels: Instagram and TikTok; Facebook supports reach, retargeting, customer proof, and older household audiences.

Content pillars:

1. **Product discovery:** new arrivals, best sellers, category edits, unboxings
2. **Value:** deals, bundles, price drops, free-delivery thresholds
3. **Trust:** packing, delivery, payment safety, returns, FAQs, reviews
4. **Utility:** comparisons, how-to-use, styling, buying guides
5. **Local relevance:** UAE occasions, weather, lifestyle, Ramadan/Eid, White Friday, National Day, DSF
6. **Community:** polls, wishlists, creator picks, UGC, review requests

### Paid social

- **Meta:** primary social acquisition and retargeting platform; use Advantage+ app campaigns or equivalent automated app promotion once events are reliable, supported by product/category creative tests.
- **TikTok:** strongest for discovery-led fashion, beauty, home, gadgets, and creator-style demonstrations. Included as a test only when the plan has enough creative volume and spend.
- Campaign layers: broad/prospecting, category/product tests, engaged-user retargeting, cart/wishlist recovery, and purchaser cross-sell.
- Creative rule: test the hook, product, offer, format, and proof—not just audience interests. Replace weak creatives quickly.

### Google Ads support

Google App campaigns can distribute ads across Google Search, YouTube, Discover, Display, and other eligible inventory. Initial campaign architecture:

- `UAE | App | OasisCart | Install-to-Purchase` — acquisition optimized first to a sufficiently frequent quality event, then first purchase
- `UAE | App | OasisCart | ReEngagement` — only after audience eligibility and enough dormant users
- Category-specific asset groups for hero categories, without fragmenting into many low-data campaigns
- Brand protection/search support only when a suitable web or deep-link destination and measurement path exist
- YouTube vertical and horizontal product demonstrations supplied to the App campaign asset library

Google management includes account setup/QA, Firebase and Google Ads linkage guidance, conversion-action design, campaign builds, asset uploads, audience signals, bid/budget management, search/category insight analysis, placement/asset review, experiments where volume allows, and monthly reporting.

### Google Merchant Center and product-feed setup

The agency will register or configure Google Merchant Center and complete the following onboarding work:

- Create/configure the Merchant Center business profile and user access
- Add the UAE business information, customer-service details, shipping settings, return policy, tax/business details, and required verification information
- Verify and claim the store website domain when a compliant e-commerce website is available
- Connect Merchant Center with Google Ads and GA4
- Select and configure the appropriate product source: scheduled feed, Google Sheets, platform integration, Content API, or automated website crawl
- Map required product information including ID, title, description, image, price, availability, brand, GTIN/MPN where applicable, condition, product URL, mobile URL, and shipping information
- Configure feed refreshes so product price and stock remain synchronized
- Review diagnostics, resolve correctable feed errors, and submit eligible products for free listings and advertising
- Establish product groups/custom labels for category, margin band, best seller, promotion, and stock priority where source data permits
- Prepare eligible catalogue campaigns after products and the destination are approved

**Important eligibility condition:** Merchant Center product listings normally require a verified, functional online store where shoppers can view the same product, price, availability, policies, and complete checkout. An App Store page alone is not a product landing page. Google also restricts marketplace models where unrelated third-party businesses sell through the site. Oasis Cart must therefore confirm whether it is the direct seller/merchant of record and provide a compliant transactional website before Shopping or catalogue-led campaigns can be guaranteed. Registration and feed preparation are included; Google approval is controlled by Google and cannot be guaranteed by the agency.

**Bidding sequence:** gather signal using install/registration only as an interim step; move to first purchase and then purchase value as volume becomes reliable. Target ROAS should not be used until purchase values are accurate and there is enough conversion history.

## 6. Measurement required before meaningful scaling

Recommended implementation: Firebase/GA4 plus an app attribution solution such as AppsFlyer, Adjust, or Singular if budget permits, with Apple AdAttributionKit/SKAdNetwork-compatible measurement.

Required events:

- First open / install
- Registration or sign-in
- Search and view item
- Add to wishlist
- Add to cart
- Begin checkout
- Add payment information
- Purchase with order ID, currency, value, product/category, and new/returning customer flag
- Refund/cancellation where technically possible

Required dashboards:

- Spend, installs, CPI, registrations, purchases, first-purchase CPA, revenue, ROAS
- AOV, gross margin or contribution margin, refund/cancellation rate
- D1/D7/D30 retention and repeat-purchase rate
- Performance by platform, campaign, emirate, language, category, product, and creative

**Primary business KPI:** allowable first-purchase CPA, calculated from gross profit after product cost, payment fees, fulfilment, delivery subsidy, refunds, discounts, and expected repeat-order value.

## 9. Performance framework and scaling rules

No agency can responsibly guarantee CPI, CPA, revenue, or ROAS before AOV, margin, conversion rate, catalogue quality, and tracking are known. The following are operating gates rather than promises:

- **Tracking gate:** purchases and values reconcile with backend orders before value-based bidding.
- **Creative gate:** a concept receives adequate spend/impressions before a decision; obvious technical or policy failures are stopped immediately.
- **Economics gate:** scale when first-purchase CPA is within the approved allowable CPA or when cohort payback supports it.
- **Scaling rule:** increase winning campaign budgets approximately 15–25% at a time; avoid sudden large increases that reset learning or reduce efficiency.
- **Retention gate:** if installs rise but registration, checkout, purchase, or repeat rates remain weak, fix the app/offer before buying more traffic.
- **Stock gate:** promote only products with accurate availability, competitive pricing, and dependable fulfilment.

Early diagnostic targets to monitor—not contractual guarantees—include:

- App Store page-to-install conversion
- Install-to-registration rate
- Registration-to-first-purchase rate
- Add-to-cart-to-purchase completion rate
- First-purchase CPA versus allowable CPA
- D30 repeat-purchase rate and cohort revenue
- Rating volume, average rating, refund/cancellation rate, and support response time

## 10. Client responsibilities

The client supplies or approves:

- Admin access to Meta Business Manager, TikTok Ads, Google Ads, App Store Connect, Firebase/GA4, and app backend reporting
- Accurate product feed, pricing, stock, margin bands, order/refund data, delivery coverage, and promotion calendar
- Brand assets, product samples, filming access, and timely approvals
- Clear shipping, return, warranty, privacy, and customer-support policies
- Developer resources for SDKs, deep links, event tracking, feeds, and technical fixes
- Customer service capable of handling comments, DMs, order issues, and increased order volume

Recommended approval SLA: two business days. Delayed approvals, missing stock data, or incomplete tracking will reduce media efficiency and content output.

## 11. Recommended next steps

1. Select the **Budgetical three-month plan** for a controlled start, with a pre-agreed option to upgrade to Medium when the tracking and allowable-CPA gates are met.
2. Hold a 90-minute commercial and technical discovery workshop.
3. Confirm AOV, gross margin, delivery fees, free-shipping threshold, payment mix, return/cancellation rate, and repeat-order expectations.
4. Audit the app funnel and implement/verify event tracking before campaign launch.
5. Select the first 10–20 hero products and agree one compelling first-order proposition.
6. Produce the first English/Arabic creative batch and improve App Store product storytelling.
7. Launch controlled campaigns, then issue the first learning report after sufficient conversion data—not simply after a fixed number of days.
